THE CASHIO DESIGN DOCTRINE

DERIVED

Rutan flew the weird thing; Johnson shipped the reliable thing. I took both and wrote them into how every node, model, and cron in ZeusApollo gets built. Six laws — no exceptions.

LAW 01

Fly It Before You Trust It

Nothing earns a place in the fleet on theory. Deploy the prototype against a real workload first — production is the only honest flight test.

FROM RUTAN · learn fast through flight test
LAW 02

One Capability, Done Cold

Every service does exactly one job and does it reliably and repeatably. No grab-bag boxes. If it can't be named in a sentence, it isn't built yet.

FROM JOHNSON · a specific capability, fast
LAW 03

Sovereign by Default

No single vendor owns a critical path. Every model has a fallback, every backup has an offsite, every DNS has a VRRP twin. Own the route, not the lock-in.

CASHIO · routed intelligence over rented
LAW 04

Earn Its Place or Get Scrapped

A node that stops paying rent in value gets decommissioned — OneDrive purged, 17 providers cut to a routed few. Lean isn't a phase; it's maintenance.

FROM RUTAN + JOHNSON · build lean, stay lean
LAW 05

Harden, Document, Ship

The moment a prototype proves itself, the discipline kicks in: CPU limits, restart guards, credentials swept, the runbook written. Then — and only then — it ships.

FROM JOHNSON · reliable, repeatable, documented
LAW 06

Lift the Iron

I don't just read about the work — I run the cluster, take the pages, and feel the failures. The homelab is the physical manifestation of that ethos.
